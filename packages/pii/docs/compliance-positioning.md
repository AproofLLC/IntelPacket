# Compliance positioning

IntelPacketPII does not make an organization compliant by itself. It provides privacy-preserving preprocessing controls that can support privacy-aligned architectures when combined with proper deployment, access control, audit logging, contractual controls, key management, retention policies, and legal review.

## What people often mean by “aligned”

Teams sometimes compare controls to well-known frameworks or regimes, for example:

- **HIPAA-aligned** healthcare architectures (e.g. PHI handling with BAAs and safeguards)  
- **GLBA-style** financial privacy programs  
- **FERPA-style** handling of education records  
- **SOC 2** security and privacy control objectives  
- **GDPR-style** internal data minimization and purpose limitation programs  

IntelPacketPII may **contribute** to technical measures in such architectures (e.g. stripping or transforming fields before storage or replication) but it is **not** a certification, audit, or legal guarantee.

## What IntelPacketPII does not claim

- No claim of **HIPAA**, **GLBA**, **FERPA**, **SOC 2**, **GDPR**, or other **compliance** or **certification**  
- No claim of being a **complete compliance program**, **identity system**, **key vault**, **database**, **AI** system, or **blockchain**  

## Practical takeaway

Treat IntelPacketPII as a **library for deterministic field transforms and reporting**, not as a substitute for organizational privacy and security governance.
